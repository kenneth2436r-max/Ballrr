⚽ About Ballrr

Ballrr started as a way to stop arguing about who owes who a game and who actually won last week's five-a-side. It's a lightweight tournament tracker built for casual football groups — friends, work leagues, weekend regulars — around one idea: keep score properly, split teams fairly, and remember who's actually good, without needing a spreadsheet or a ref with a clipboard.

Everything works fully offline on your device by default. Sign in with Google only if you want a tournament synced across your own devices, or shared live with your group.

Tutorial & FAQ

Tournament mode runs your full multi-team competition — league table, knockout bracket, draft, stats, the works. Quick Match (top of the screen) is a fast single 2-team scoreboard for a one-off kickabout. It doesn't touch your tournament data at all, and switching back to Tournament mode restores everything exactly where you left it.

The toggle in Settings → Tournament Mode isn't just wording. Friendly mode keeps the fun extras: templated match recaps, the Rivalry Meter, confetti and fanfare on results. Competitive mode swaps those for organizer tools: the RSVP & free-agent pool, QR check-in, and the Fee & Expense Ledger. Player cards, archetypes, the Trophy Cabinet, and balanced draft mode stay available in both, since those are useful either way.

Add players to the pool in the Draft tab, then spin — each spin randomly assigns the next player to whichever team is due a pick, following turn order (snake order if you've turned that on). Switch on "Balanced draft" and it leans on each player's saved rating history to even out squad strength instead of picking purely at random. "Auto-fill" skips the animation and instantly assigns everyone left in the pool.

League runs a round-robin points table (single, double, triple or quadruple round robin) with an optional playoff at the end — the Table tab shows live standings throughout. Knockout skips the group stage entirely: teams go straight into a seeded single-elimination bracket from Round 1. Pick either in Settings → Tournament Format; in Knockout format the Table tab shows a live bracket tree instead of standings, with eliminated teams crossed out and winners carried forward.

Yes, in League format only. Settings → Custom Knockout Format lets you define your own stages (say, Quarterfinal → Semifinal → Final) and wire each match to a specific league rank, or to the winner/loser of an earlier stage — instead of relying on the automatic seeded bracket.

Settings → Shared Tournament → Share. "Open" sharing lets anyone with the code join instantly. "Approval" sharing (Competitive mode only) means new joiners wait in a pending screen until you approve them. Everyone with access sees the same live data — results, table, draft, everything — synced in real time.

Once a tournament is shared, open the Draft tab in Competitive mode — the RSVP/Guest List section there gets a "📱 Show Check-in QR" button. Anyone who scans it can mark themselves present or add themselves as a free agent, without you managing it by hand. If you don't see the button, you're either still in Friendly mode or haven't shared the tournament yet — both are required.

Both only appear on shared tournaments. Before a match kicks off, anyone with access can submit a scoreline prediction — 3 points for an exact score, 1 for just the correct result. After a match, players vote for their pick for Player of the Match, and the tally shows right on the match card.

A Competitive-mode tally in Settings for tracking who's paid their entry fee and logging shared costs like ground booking. It's just bookkeeping — nothing here moves real money.

Tap any player's name to open their card. Archetypes are worked out from their stat history — goals, assists, clean sheets, ratings — across your saved tournaments. The Trophy Cabinet retroactively tallies up Golden Boot, Playmaker, Golden Glove and Player of the Match wins.

Archive tab → Save Tournament first, which locks the current tournament's stats into your Career/Archive history. Then Settings → Reset Tournament clears the board for a new one. Reset keeps your player stats and archive; it does not carry over RSVP data, since that's meant to be a fresh guest list each time.
